Food Insecurity

Our Commitment to Alleviate Food Insecurity

Maize & Blue Cupboard: Visit our on-campus food pantry, the Maize & Blue Cupboard, which provides free groceries and personal care items to students in need. Students can shop for fresh produce, dairy, grains, proteins, and more in a supportive and welcoming environment.

Meal Sharing Programs: Participate in programs like "Go Blue Guarantee," which allow students to donate unused meal swipes to peers in need. These programs aim to ensure every student has access to regular meals.

Emergency Financial Assistance: Apply for emergency funds through the Dean of Students Office to help manage unexpected expenses, including food costs, that could impact your academic stability.

Nutrition and Budgeting Workshops: Attend workshops that provide tips on budgeting, meal planning, and cooking nutritious meals on a limited budget. Learn from experts about maximizing your food options while maintaining a healthy diet.

Campus Dining Flexibility: Explore flexible dining plan options that cater to diverse dietary needs and preferences, making it easier to manage your food resources effectively.
